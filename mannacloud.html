MannaCloud

Founder, Branding

Project Summary

My fiancé and I founded MannaCloud in October, 2016. It is a small company that provides online services to churches, from design to hosting. So far, I’ve managed to create the brand and overall design for the website.

I started with the name, MannaCloud. I experimented with many types of logos, originally with a sky-blue primary color. I tried a variety of cloud silhouettes, until I finally settled on a style that was sharper. Since the rounded text didn’t really fit with the new style, I moved onto a more basic font for the type.

Next, I experimented with colors. I narrowed it down to two: Professional Blue, or Creative Purple. Since this was a creative agency, I was already leaning towards purple. I played around with different palettes and finally settled on some muted purples, with a complimentary teal accent.

While I was working on the wireframes, I took some time to do some low-fidelity color mockups. I wanted to make sure the way I was using the colors looked and felt comfortable throughout the composition. I then translated this to the final mockup. Revisions are still being made, but overall is heading in the right direction.

I’m really looking forward to continuing work on MannaCloud in the future. I’m hoping it can be a fulfilling side project that I can continue to develop in my spare time.
